# Completeness critique — EFS v2 design round (2026-07-07)

**Role:** completeness critic. I read all nine work products + five attack reports + the handoff/settled-direction ground truth, hunted for surfaces nobody designed, adjudicated cross-doc contradictions, and audited the red teams' severity calls (including two greps into the primary specs to confirm a contradiction no red team caught pairwise).

**Verdict up front:** the round holds. The settled direction (native kernel, chain-free envelope, tag-core, 5 kinds, bare-EOA + reservations) survived five independent red teams, two of which verified the crypto **in code** (constants, signatures, Merkle fuzz, vectors — all byte-exact). There is no true fatal anywhere. What stands between this corpus and a freeze-shaped spec: **one reconciliation ruling** (a cluster of consensus-bearing constants and semantics on which sibling docs diverged inside the same session), **one new Etched invariant** (admission confluence), **two missing documents** (the read/lens spec; the deterministic-ids v2 delta), **five James ratifications**, and the **unrun executable checks** (gas, EIP-170 compile, vectors, fire drill). Everything else is doc text.

## 1. GAPS — what the whole round missed

### G1 (the big one). No read/lens-resolution spec exists, and half the round's fixes route into it
The lens model (per-viewer ordered trusted-author lists, first-attester-wins) is THE read-side primitive of the mission and the settled direction makes read-grade vocabulary **normative** — yet no document in the round designs read resolution. Evidence of the hole: ops-honesty explicitly defers deny-semantics "to the lens resolution spec — flagged upward, not resolved" (ops §1.4/§7.1) and its red team shows the deferral is **load-bearing for the flagship package-registry case** (attack-ops D3); attack-kinds K6 needs a fallthrough context split that belongs in a read spec; attack-ops E1/E2/E4/E5 add grades (EQUIVOCAL, SUPERSEDED, bytes-unavailable, venue-qualified STALE) to a table no spec owns; kinds-ruling §2.7/§4.2 mandates a "read-precedence chapter with vectors" that has no home; the container classifier (G4) is read surface. The doc is Durable, not Etched — but until it exists, "normative read grades" is a phrase, not a spec, and conformance tests (anti-fallthrough, pin-and-diff) have nothing to test against. **Action: commission the Read & Lens Spec as a first-class deliverable of the synthesis; adopt client-side deny-filter composition (advisory lenses subtract after allow-resolution) as the v2 answer so nothing kernel-side blocks on it.**

### G2. The container-scoped cross-author discovery index was deliberately not ruled — and then nobody ruled it
Consumer apps demand #1 (apps 2 & 3 hinge on it); kinds-ruling §4.4a/§11.7 calls it "the single highest-leverage open item" and explicitly defers to the kernel-index ruling; native-kernel.md (written the same hour) only re-ratifies the v1 keep/demote line (§251) and never sees the new demand. It is **kernel index surface = Etched**; freezing reads without deciding it ratifies indexer-dependence for two of ten apps by default — an Etched decision being made by silence. Both app passes lean "add it" (bounded, per-tagId, paginated, spam-absorbed at writer's gas, labeled enumeration ≠ endorsement). It is also a real per-write cost and interacts with the EIP-170 budget → bundle with the spine for James (see §6).

### G3. The deterministic-ids v2 delta is unconsolidated — "port, don't re-derive" is eroding by accretion
At least seven amendments to the frozen ID math are scattered across five docs, none owned: (1) claimId domain + formula (C1 below); (2) listId config-fold (C7); (3) carve-out extension to TAG-role + ADDRESS-parent rows (kinds §9.2); (4) closed targetKind enumeration + the OPAQUE rule (attack-kinds K5); (5) NFC/name-profile enforcement mechanism (attack-envelope C4 — vector 22 vs on-chain-NFC infeasibility; likely answer: restrict the profile to a codepoint set where NFC == identity); (6) TAGDEF domain-constant naming + KIND_PROPERTY naming skew (envelope routing; kinds §5); (7) home/successor rows. Each alone is fine; unconsolidated, they are exactly the forSchema-class drift the Codex discipline exists to prevent. **Action: a single "deterministic-ids v2 delta" chapter, one owner, re-vectored once.**

### G4. Container classifier / URL surface was never redesigned for the post-EAS world
The handoff lists it as a [reasoned] leaning with unproven 64-hex collision-safety; the classifier's classes (Address > Schema > Attestation > Tag) are half-dead with EAS gone (what are the v2 container classes — address word, tagId, dataId, listId?). Hyperlinkable-across-chains is a mission end; consumer app 2 hit the 64-hex segment-name collision; attack-kinds S11 (file-URLs as hostile container prefixes) lands here too. Nobody in the round touched it. Read-spec chapter (Durable) + one derivation question (does an explicit-prefix escape hatch exist?) that should be answered before URLs appear in any Codex example.

### G5. The frozen read ABI + state-enumerability doctrine has no single owner
Three passes levy read-surface requirements no one consolidated: envelope FM15 routes "per-slot active-claim enumeration" to the kernel; identity E2 levies two state requirements (admission order state-walk-reconstructible; superseded claims state-enumerable) for the 2030 union-read rules; infra demand #2 ruled bodies-in-state normative; kernel evicts enumerating views to redeployable contracts. Which functions are Etched-on-kernel vs view-contract, and what the spine does vs per-slot indices, needs one statement — especially since C8 (below) shows the two primary specs currently disagree about what a slot read even returns after a revoke.

### G6. Genesis manifest contents unspecified
Kernel ruled the mechanics (hash-pinned idempotent blob, SYSTEM_AUTHOR, Codex at CREATE2 chunks) but no one enumerated WHAT is in the blob: which TAGDEFs (reserved-key rows? /transports docs?), which objects, the genesis pseudo-header for claimIds (attack-kernel S3), chunked init for low-gas-cap chains. Etched content with no manifest.

### G7. The commissioned executable checks were not run — and several verdicts are hostage to them
Unrun: (a) the gas benchmark — every table in the corpus is flagged estimate, two app verdicts hostage; (b) the **EIP-170 skeleton compile** — attack-kernel shows the named fallback ladder's first rung is a compiler no-op and the one-vs-two-Etched-artifacts question is a coin flip discoverable in a day; (c) golden-vector generation (enumerations are normative, the numbers are not); (d) the dead-chain fire drill (the 100-year offline-verify claim is explicitly conditional on it); (e) force-inclusion/per-chain verification of the [training-knowledge] rows; (f) legal review of operator tiers. None is design work; all gate freeze. (Note: attack-envelope substantially discharged the "envelope+Merkle rehearsal" — the crypto was re-implemented and fuzzed.)

### G8. Visibility machinery fate unadjudicated
v1 visibility propagation is mentioned in the kinds re-homing table ("visibility propagation → edge module") and consumer apps treated it as app-invisible, but no one ruled whether the v1 visibility-TAG system survives, becomes a contains-walk, or dies. It is kernel index/write-path surface (Etched) and it appears in the per-record gas anatomy. Small, but it is an Etched default being made by inheritance.

### G9. Encryption/key-wrap coupling to the identity key — one missing sentence with retroactive blast radius
attack-identity A3: if keyWraps target the identity key, THEFT = retroactive decryption of the entire archive (the only non-monotone consequence in the system) and LOSS = author loses own data. The reserved keys (contentEncryption/keyWrap) exist; the rule ("key-wrap targets MUST be independent of the identity key" — or an explicit admission of the coupling) exists nowhere. One normative sentence, but somebody must own the (reserved) encryption conventions.

### G10. Housekeeping the handoff asked for and nobody picked up
(a) disposition of the v1 devnet/prototype; (b) the written scope of the one-final-freeze pledge — the round generated dozens of "freeze-blocking" items with no consolidated gate list or definition of Etched vs Durable (fixed by D9 below); (c) the Harberger-overlay/lens-neutrality interface. Also one ungrounded app class, named honestly everywhere: the numeric-consumer marketplace that could flip string-only — the re-check trigger stands; acceptable.

## 3. FATAL TRIAGE — the check on the checkers

Only one finding in the round was labeled fatal-tier; I audited it plus the credibility of the zero-fatal verdicts and hunted for under-calls.

1. **attack-ops E6 (checkpoint dependency) — REAL as a freeze-blocking reconciliation, OVERCLAIMED as "conditionally fatal."** The dependency is genuine and the ops doc's silence was a defect, but the favorable reading was already in the corpus (kinds §1.5 reserves CHECKPOINT) and the C4 resolution (checkpoint as an ordinary reserved-key claim, zero machinery) repairs it with one paragraph + one James sentence. Nothing about the settled direction is threatened.
2. **The zero-fatal verdicts on envelope and identity are CREDIBLE at the highest available standard** — both red teams re-derived constants, reproduced EIP-712 digests/signatures byte-for-byte against independent tooling, and fuzzed the Merkle construction. These are executed verifications, not opinions. The direction's crypto core should be treated as standing.
3. **UNDER-CALL (the one I insist on): the admission-confluence cluster is collectively fatal-grade if shipped unfixed, though no single report said so.** attack-kernel §2 (N-set checks reading revocable state → honest-author permanent divergence), attack-kinds K2 (selective replication-coherence; chain-selective envelopes), and attack-envelope B1 (maxEntries admission gate violating the spec's own invariant 4) are the SAME disease found in three corners, each filed as SERIOUS in isolation. Jointly they break write-once-copy-anywhere — the mission-critical property — for honest authors, no adversary needed. The fix (C6) is one Etched invariant + three mechanical changes, cheap this week, impossible after vectors. The synthesis must not let severity-by-report-fragmentation bury it: it is fix #1.
4. **attack-kernel D7's recommended winner is likely WRONG** (the finding — a contradiction exists — is real). It sided with kernel's revoke-target-must-exist against three sibling docs; the G-set model dissolves its spam/author-check objection (C3). Do not adopt a red team's resolution as if it were the adjudication.
5. **attack-identity E1 is PARTIALLY OVERCLAIMED**: envelope §5.7 already intrinsic-rejects unknown/reserved kinds and kernel R2 reserves reverting kindCodes — the rule exists in two siblings; identity.md just didn't cite it. Keep the one-line closed-list `ReservedKindTag` rule; no new machinery. (The red team read only its target file — a process note for future rounds.)
6. **attack-kinds K1/K2 are REAL and correctly severity'd freeze-blocking**; K1 in particular (expiresAt hollowing appendOnly) voids the stated reason LIST was kept and its one-require fix must ship with C5.
7. **Suppressed fatals: none found.** My independent hunt across pairwise doc seams produced exactly one contradiction the red teams missed (C8 — real, but convergence-safe under either semantics; a pick, not a hole) and one structurally unattacked layer (the read/lens layer — unattacked because undesigned, which is G1, not a hidden fatal).

## 5. Open questions — James vs synthesis

## 6. Limitations of this critique
I read all five attack reports in full, the kinds ruling in full, the handoff, and targeted sections of the primary specs (grep-verified C8, G2, G1); the envelope/kernel/identity/ops/app files I otherwise consumed via their digests plus the red teams' section-cited quotations.
