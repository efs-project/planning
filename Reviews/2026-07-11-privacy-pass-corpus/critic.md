## 2. Cross-lane conflict adjudications

**C-A. Occurrence-key wording: "random" (FS-pass E5 / metadata) vs "opaque" (layer1 F-3). WINNER: OPAQUE.**
The FS-pass reserved E5 as "random-default occurrence keys." layer1 F-3 shows the frozen row text MUST say **opaque — computationally unlinkable to recipient identity by non-holders (random being the degenerate case)**, because the scan lanes it designs (H1 view-tags, H2 PRF-derived pairwise mailboxes) and the owner self-escrow key (F-4, HKDF-derived) are *structured, not random* — a frozen "MUST be random" makes the entire scanning solution, the owner-recovery path, and any future FMD upgrade **non-conforming forever**. law-positioning R8 (self-escrow HKDF-derivable, or walkaway WA-1 fails) and attack-os-tier converge on the same point. This is load-bearing freeze wording and it refines, not contradicts, the A1 oracle-closure ruling (the point was always *unlinkable*, and "random" was shorthand). **Ruling: E5 row text = "opaque," random is one instance.** In the consolidated table as F-3.

**C-B. The scan/discovery problem is ONE problem across two lanes. WINNER: unify.**
layer1 §2 (keyWrap recipient discovery) and frontier-stealth §B4 (announcement scanning) are the same problem — find records addressed to me among opaque-keyed records I can't point-read. layer1 explicitly says "if the stealth lane reserves an announcement-hint format, the keyWrap hint format should be the same format — one scanning pipeline, two record families." Both suffer the permanent-feed scan-cost problem (SF-1). **Ruling: one scan convention** — incremental cursor (always) + pairwise mailboxes for known counterparties (zero-leak, H2) + view-tag scan for stranger shares/announcements (H1, k=16) + self-index escrow so recovery never replays history + self-fleets/self-derivation avoiding scanning entirely for own content. The stealth genesis feed, IF reserved, gets an epoch field so the incremental cursor works (SF-1 repair). Neither needs an Etched index (point-reads can't serve hidden recipients by definition; scanning rides events + spine + P8 snapshots — B7's REJECT stands).

**C-C. Stealth freeze surface: three-way conflict. WINNER: minimal set, most items deferrable.**
- frontier-stealth: mint `stealthMeta` ROW + genesis announce TAGDEF + schemeTag registry family.
- metadata-adversary R1: REJECT a dedicated stealth row — ride the existing `encryptionKey` C3 row + KEM algoTag; reject the derivation domain; the only now-or-never bit is keeping the KEM algoTag space open-ended.
- law-positioning R6: reserve the minimal hedge (`stealthMeta` row + derivation-domain constant) only if the frontier lane doesn't converge.
- attack-frontier: KEEP the `stealthMeta` row (verified sufficient), KEEP/soften the genesis feed, DOWNGRADE the schemeTag registry to Durable (OR-1), keep derivation-domain Durable.

Adjudication on the substance: a new reserved-key **ROW** is ceremony-only (can't be added post-freeze); a new **algoTag/role inside the `encryptionKey` blob** is Durable (addable post-freeze) *if the blob grammar is open-ended*. metadata-adversary is therefore correct that, **given F-5 (open-ended typed-multi-key `encryptionKey` blob), a stealth meta-address is a post-freeze-addable blob entry** — so the dedicated `stealthMeta` row is NOT now-or-never. The genesis announce anchor IS now-or-never (frozen `efs.well-known` membership). The schemeTag registry is Durable (OR-1). The derivation domain is Durable (client-side EC math; kernel recomputes nothing — R4/metadata-R1, and this KILLS privacy.md §9's "reserved derivation-domain, now-or-never" hedge).
**Ruling / pass recommendation:**
1. **Confirm F-5 (open-ended `encryptionKey` blob + KEM registry).** Needed anyway (scan-key role, PQ migration); it makes stealth-meta publication a Durable addition. This is the linchpin.
2. **Genesis stealth announce TAGDEF: JAMES DECISION (JD-8)** — reserve one manifest line WITH an epoch field (SF-1), OR skip and use a reshardable post-freeze convention feed. Self-fleets need no feed; announced stealth is the only consumer, and it is a rare-invite primitive. Reserving is cheap; do not let the "strongest now-or-never" rhetoric drive it.
3. **Dedicated `stealthMeta` row: DEFER** (addable via the open blob per F-5). James may mint it for a clean Schelling point (the red team verified it sufficient), but it is not now-or-never. Recommendation: don't mint it; use the blob.
4. **schemeTag registry ROW → DOWNGRADE to Durable** (OR-1). Reserve only "stealth blobs are scheme-tagged in a namespace distinct from the signature and KEM registries" (the S1 category-error lesson).

**C-D. ZK freeze surface. WINNER: consensus — zero new rows.**
frontier-zk and metadata-adversary agree: group roots/commitments are ordinary VAL claims under permissionless user key-TAGDEFs; nullifiers are sibling-contract or read-layer evidence; verification is client-side or sibling-contract; the master invariant (already frozen) forbids write-time gates and thus admission-time ZK. Native group authorship rides the already-reserved digest-shaped author space + peer-kernel path. **KILLS privacy.md §9's "reserved commitment/nullifier row" hedge.** The only ceremony-batch edit is R5-zk (key-privacy sentence in the KEM registry text), which rides the C3/E5 batch and also serves attack-privacy S1. The R1-zk "ZK never gates admission" sentence is invariant-commentary (ZF-3), not new surface.

**C-E. keyWrap dual-role PIN. WINNER: stays dead (TAG-only).**
The FS pass C6 killed the dual-role PIN; layer1 §1.6 re-confirms TAG-only with random/opaque occurrence keys. No lane reopened it. On the KILL LIST as a reinstatement guard.

**C-F. Private-folder construction: salted TAGDEFs (FS-pass D3) vs encrypted dirnodes (layer1 §5.3). WINNER: encrypted dirnodes as the default.**
Adjudicated in §1 W2/W3. Encrypted dirnodes ship at launch (zero freeze surface), leak only one object's existence+cadence+padded-size (vs salted trees' full topology + per-node timing), cost O(1) records per folder, and dodge the post-freeze-activation trap. Salted TAGDEFs remain the reserved addressable/disclosable tier (their F-1/F-2 derivation math still owed since the family is kept). This is a default flip, not a deletion.

**C-G. claimedAt=0 as timing privacy. WINNER: it is NOT timing privacy (near-theater).**
metadata Playbook 2 and the attack-os-tier canon check agree: `order`/TID already carries author wall-clock microseconds, mandatory and signed, so zeroing `claimedAt` removes only a redundant copy. The real (weak) lever is client-side TID coarsening at a supersession-priority cost; `admittedAt` is an unbeatable coarse floor. **Ruling: keep the `claimedAt=0` rider but correct A2/F13 row text to say it is NOT timing privacy.** Refinement, on the KILL LIST as a downgraded claim.

**C-H. Persona-link visibility (public D2 pair vs private fleet map). WINNER: public personas use the D2 rows; private fleet trust is content, never a claim.**
frontier-stealth R5 rejects a private persona-link row (a slot keyed on the primary leaks existence+count of hidden personas — the A1 oracle twin). Private fleet maps live as encrypted DATA. Consistent with attack-os-tier §4 and the D2 reservation (public personas only). No conflict; resolves privacy.md §11's open persona-link-visibility question for the *private* case: private links are content, not rows.

**C-I. Fileverse as ZK-membership production proof. WINNER: it is NOT (see Kill List).**
frontier-zk §1.2 (VERIFIED, every source file) + autopsies + attack-frontier: the shipped `zk-granular-permissions` has no SNARK anywhere — it is vOPRF + Merkle-as-key-derivation + AES. privacy.md §7/§9/§10's "ZK-membership live production instance" framing is false; the real mechanism is OPRF-hardened key distribution, which EFS's random-occurrence-key + published-high-entropy-`encryptionKey` design already dominates on EFS's threat model. This removes a false premise that could have justified aggressive ZK reservations.

## 4. The consolidated James decision list (ordered by consequence)

Plain-English, example, options, recommendation, defer-cost. Merged and deduped across all lanes.

**JD-1 — Split the private tier: `private-recoverable` vs `private-shreddable`.**
Plain: you cannot promise both "get everything back after losing all your devices" AND "crypto-shred means truly gone" for the *same* key — a key you can recover from nothing-you-hold is a key that can never be permanently destroyed. Example: your tax archive you want recoverable forever (escrow/phrase-backed, not shreddable); a leaked-source document you must be able to destroy (enclave-bound key, no escrow, lose-your-device-lose-it). Options: (a) ship the split, user picks per file/subtree; (b) one "private tier" implying both — dishonest; (c) recoverable-only, no real shred. **Recommend (a)**, default new personal files to `private-recoverable`. Defer-cost: the positioning FAQ and the century-storage audit already flagged this; shipping one tier that implies both is a documented contradiction a competent critic will expose.

**JD-2 — Adopt the positioning statement (law §1) as official words.** Plain: the exact README/FAQ/one-liner sentences about privacy and deletion. Example: "can I delete?" always gets revoke / crypto-shred / can't-unsay, verbatim. Options: (a) adopt as-is; (b) adopt the banned/required-word rules but let docs paraphrase; (c) defer. **Recommend (a)** — see §7 for the blessing/markups. Defer-cost: improvised marketing is legal surface (Samourai's copy was read in court).

**JD-3 — Committing AEAD MUST for the private tier.** Plain: ordinary encryption (AES-GCM) lets a savvy attacker build one file that decrypts to *different documents for different people*, both "verifying" as the same fileId — breaking EFS's "one fileId = one content" promise. Committing encryption (same standard parts, ~zero cost) + a 32-byte key-fingerprint in every header and share makes it impossible and auto-rejects poisoned shares. Options: (a) plain AES-GCM; (b) committing MUST + `dekCommit`; (c) per-app optional. **Recommend (b)** — cheapest place in the design to buy a real guarantee. Defer-cost: the byte-identity promise becomes app-dependent and unverifiable from outside.

**JD-4 — X-Wing PQ hybrid now, don't wait for the RFC.** Plain: every key-share on-chain is quantum-harvestable forever; the fix (X25519+ML-KEM = X-Wing) is standardized-in-substance but the IETF stamp isn't final. Options: (a) ship X-Wing under our own algoTag `0x01` with pinned vectors (RFC byte-shift → `0x02` later); (b) wait (every meanwhile-wrap is classical-harvestable or blocked); (c) classical now — REJECTED (can't un-harvest). **Recommend (a).**

**JD-5 — Scan/discovery + occurrence-key wording.** Plain: because recipient names are hidden (correctly), phones must *discover* incoming shares; naive scan dies at scale. Options: (a) naive; (b) three lanes (incremental cursor + pairwise mailboxes + view-tag stranger-scan + self-index recovery), unified with stealth scanning; (c) delegate to an indexer (opt-in, leaks your receipt pattern). **Recommend (b) with (c) opt-in.** Requires freeze wording F-3 ("opaque" not "random") or lanes 2/3 are illegal forever.

**JD-6 — Private-folder default: encrypted dirnodes (launch) vs salted trees (post-freeze).** Plain: option A (salted trees) shows the chain an opaque cluster ("something with 40 children edited Tuesdays") AND its resolver only activates post-freeze — so private *folders* wouldn't ship at launch. Option B (encrypted dirnode) is one encrypted file listing its children: the chain sees one object + edit cadence, no tree shape, ~40× fewer records, and ships day one. Options: (a) salted default; (b) encrypted-dirnode default, salted trees for the narrow addressable/disclosable tier. **Recommend (b)** — less leakage, less gas, same sharing power, and it fixes the launch-availability gap. State the tiering: confidential *files* + wrapped caps at launch; salted private *folders/links* are a reserved post-freeze-activation capability. Defer-cost: promising day-one salted private directories is vaporware (writes against a dead resolver).

**JD-7 — Master secret origin: random root + device-wrap backup, signature-derivation banned.** Plain: deriving your master secret from a wallet signature ("sign to unlock") feels magical but any dapp that gets you to sign that message OWNS your whole private archive forever, unrotatably — and some signers aren't deterministic (data lost). Options: (a) signature-derived (the Fileverse trap); (b) random root, backed up by wrapping to your other devices + optional passphrase recovery; (c) (b) with a scoped EIP-712 escape hatch. **Recommend (b)**, (c) only if onboarding friction bites. Ratify: G9 extends — no root secret derivable from the identity key or its signatures.

**JD-8 — Stealth freeze set (the now-or-never subset).** Plain: to allow unlinkable pen-name authorship later, the only genuinely permanent thing is *maybe* one "well-known feed" so everyone scans one place for "I left something for you" notices (a single permanent feed is also a spam-scan magnet). The stealth-key slot rides the encryption-key blob you're already opening (F-5), so it needs no separate row; the derivation math is client-side. Options: (a) reserve the genesis announce feed WITH an epoch field (bounds spam-scan, keeps canonical anonymity set); (b) skip it — self-fleets need no feed, announced stealth uses a reshardable post-freeze convention feed; (c) mint a full dedicated stealth row + registry — **over-reservation, not recommended**. **Recommend (a) if canonical announced stealth is wanted, else (b).** Do NOT mint the schemeTag registry as a row (Durable) and do NOT mint the derivation domain. Defer-cost: only the genesis feed is now-or-never; everything else is addable later.

**JD-9 — Default-on stealth meta-address at OS onboarding — gated on the scan-DoS.** Plain: publishing a stealth slot is a public "I can receive private stuff" flag; if only enthusiasts do it, being on the list is itself revealing, but default-on maximizes the crowd — AND the scan cost every device pays forever AND the quantum-retro-linkage blast radius. Options: (a) default-on; (b) opt-in; (c) defer to OS pass. **Recommend deferring to the OS pass (JD-1 tier work), but price the scan-DoS and the CRQC blast radius first** (a default-on user carries a permanent public view key, so every announcement to any default-on user becomes retro-linkable at CRQC).

**JD-10 — Groups: pairwise + team-key now, no MLS on-chain.** Plain: removing someone from a 10-person folder = ~9 re-shares (pennies); at 100 it's noticeable; MLS/TreeKEM only pays off in the hundreds-with-churn and needs a sequencer EFS lacks. Options: (a) pairwise + team-key indirection, CGKA client-side later; (b) build TreeKEM now. **Recommend (a).** Ratify the honesty rider: EFS never claims "forward secrecy" (impossible on a permanent archive) — it claims forward-only re-key + shred.

**JD-11 — Recovery ladder + share UX.** Plain: your signing key is unrecoverable (until the KEL), but your *encrypted data* is recoverable if you make one artifact at setup — and users must hear the account-vs-data distinction before disaster (Proton's rule). Also: raw "share links" leak into clipboard-sync/history-sync/message stores, so durable shares must be wrapped to the recipient, not bearer caps in a URL. Options: (a) phrase (mandatory prompt) + `.efs-bundle` file + opt-in social (archive-root only, never shred-root), and composer upgrades raw caps pasted into synced surfaces; (b) phrase + file only; (c) file only. **Recommend (a).**

**JD-12 — Walkaway test as a shipped CI conformance gate.** Plain: a CI job proving, every release, that a user with only their keys + any public RPC gets everything back — and that the *holes* (revoked, shredded, never-accepted shares) are exactly the documented ones. Options: (a) WA-0..3 hard gates + WA-4 annual public ritual; (b) WA-0..1 at launch; (c) manual page only. **Recommend (a)** — it is the mechanical proof of the positioning claims and the executable spec of the (non-frozen) privacy conventions.

**JD-13 — Read-path SDK default.** Plain: whoever provides your chain connection sees everything you read + your IP. Options: (a) silent big provider (what wallets do); (b) no silent default: first-run picker + always-on embedded light client (integrity), OHTTP client half built in; (c) (b) + OHTTP toggle now (but no relay exists to point it at). **Recommend (b)** — matches "EFS working well first; privacy secondary." Note rung 2 is dark until someone stands up and *sustains* a relay/gateway pair (RPCh died of no funding).

**JD-14 — Who publishes bulk snapshots.** Plain: the floor already requires lens/deny/index/checkpoint bundles; someone must build them nightly. Options: (a) document format only; (b) EFS.eth publishes reference snapshots, digest-anchored, anyone mirrors; (c) fund ≥2 independent publishers. **Recommend (b) now, (c) when a community exists.**

**JD-15 — PIR: shelf with a named trigger.** Plain: private lookups where even the server can't tell what you asked (Apple ships this). Fits EFS point-reads, needs zero frozen surface. Options: (a) shelf, revisit when the slot DB passes ~5–10 GB or a phone-first client ships without local snapshots; (b) post-hackathon pilot; (c) launch-blocking. **Recommend (a) with (b) as the named next step.**

**JD-16 — Gateway posture / operator doctrine.** Plain: any EFS-run service re-enters ordinary law (takedowns, sanctions screening, NCMEC). Options: (a) code-only, run nothing but the devnet; (b) code + one demo gateway under a *separate legal entity* with a published minimal-log policy + staffed notice pipeline; (c) + an "official" relayer — REJECTED (a fee/discretion relayer is a control point; Storm's §1960 count). **Recommend (b) only if the pipeline is actually staffed, else (a).** Never custody, never a fee-taking discretionary relayer, never kernel moderation.

**JD-17 — GDPR posture.** Plain: compliance is about what *you* publish, not a protocol property; the EU regulators (EDPB, July 2026) say personal data — plain, encrypted, or hashed — shouldn't go on a public chain at all. Options: (a) ratify "EFS is not a home for others' personal data," teach off-chain+commitment as *the* pattern, add the GDPR + takedown FAQs, describe crypto-shred as the strongest-available erasure-equivalent (contested as legal erasure); (b) ratify but keep GDPR out of the FAQ; (c) wait for a legal opinion. **Recommend (a).**

**JD-18 — Horizon calibration.** Plain: how much privacy work to fund, and which half. Transport/funding/read-path genuinely arrive from upstream Ethereum (Kohaku-class wallets, shielded gas, FOCIL) — ride them, don't build. The NEVER rows (graph shape, authorship-at-record-layer, recipient sets, sizes, shred) are EFS-owned and no future Ethereum touches them. Options: (a) confirm the split; (b) confirm + fund one metadata mitigation this year; (c) treat all privacy as post-launch — REJECTED (the freeze-sensitive subset can't move post-ceremony). **Recommend (a).**

**JD-19 — Private files carry no plaintext labels.** Plain: a private file publicly saying `name=divorce-settlement.pdf, size=1.2MB` has leaked the point. Options: (a) MUST — private-tier files assert no `name`/`size`/`contentType` rows, use normalized chunk/padding, OS does it automatically; (b) SHOULD guidance; (c) allow a padded-bucket `size` row for UX with the leak named. **Recommend (a)** (with (c) only if the OS needs approximate-size sorting).

**JD-20 — Unlinkable-persona default + wrong-persona guard.** Plain: an "unlinkable" persona is only unlinkable if (private link) AND (relayed/sponsored flush) AND (no funding from a linked wallet) — private link alone collapses on funding provenance. And a promptless write into a private subtree under the wrong (public) persona silently authors a private file under your real identity. Options: (a) default unlinkable personas to sponsored/relayed flush + refuse/loud-warn on linked-wallet top-up, AND break the promptless path with a loud guard for private-tier writes under a public persona; (b) warnings only. **Recommend (a)** — a warning is not a default.

**JD-21 — Re-key default: lazy vs eager, priced at removal.** Plain: removing someone lazily (re-key only on next edit) is cheap but leaves every never-again-edited file readable by them forever; eager re-key is clean but costs O(files×members), and the gas gradient silently pushes users toward the leaky mode. Options: (a) explicit per-removal choice with the security consequence AND cost stated ("clean now ~$X; or free lazy, K cold files stay readable by the removed member"); (b) silent lazy default. **Recommend (a).** Surface the forward-only law at *removal*, not just grant.

**JD-22 — Timing posture (honest vs coarse TID).** Plain: every record's `order` word is a microsecond wall-clock stamp leaking your timezone/schedule; `claimedAt=0` does NOT fix this. Options: (a) honest TID everywhere; (b) coarse TID everywhere (weaker supersession); (c) per-tier — honest in the public archive, coarsened in the private/OS tier (with the supersession cost), and document that `admittedAt` is an unbeatable floor and `claimedAt=0` is not timing privacy. **Recommend (c).**

**JD-23 — Viewing-key ritual as a named feature.** Plain: Zcash's most durable idea is a read-only key you hand an auditor (they see scope, change nothing, you chose the scope). EFS has the parts (per-folder salts + disclosure records). Options: (a) name it ("Disclosure Key for /taxes/2026"), document it, add to the share sheet; (b) leave implicit. **Recommend (a)** — selective disclosure unnamed is selective disclosure unused.

**JD-24 — Ban on-chain read receipts / "seen" markers (ratify the REJECT).** Plain: Signal was de-anonymized in ~5 messages by automatic "delivered" echoes; on EFS such an echo is public and permanent. Options: (a) hard REJECT any reception-echo record; (b) opt-in public acks with a warning — REJECTED (defaults drift). **Recommend (a)**; acks/presence live on the off-chain relay only.

**JD-25 — Fleet-trust in client-config lenses (carve out of LC2).** Plain: if you trust an anonymous "fleet" of your own pen-names via a *published* lens, that lens publicly lists and clusters the fleet — defeating the point. Options: (a) fleet-trust lives only in per-viewer local lens config, explicitly exempt from LC2's "default lens must be published"; a shared workspace's public lens names only the container author; (b) allow published fleet lenses. **Recommend (a).**
