# 2026-07-02 — Substrate investigation record (EAS vs native vs portable records)

**Context:** James reopened the foundation question — EFS's EAS usage kept shrinking through the v2 design rounds, and EAS binds even offchain attestations to a chainId. Mission fixed, all means open (EAS itself, all ADRs, no timeline). This record documents the investigation behind [[efs-substrate-decision]]. Full corpus (13 research autopsies + 4 architecture files, ~850KB): `planning/Reviews/2026-07-02-substrate-corpus/`.

**Method:** 25 agents — 13 parallel research autopsies (writing full reports to files) → 4 architects (each reading all research, designing one end-to-end candidate) → 4 red teams (one per architecture) → 3 value-system judges (archival / builder / cypherpunk) → 1 completeness critic adjudicating.

## Research phase — the decisive facts

- **Nostr** proves the portable-signature goal (chain-free event IDs, verify-anywhere) and the costs of nothing-else: advisory deletion, LWW on self-asserted timestamps, relay decay (95% of free relays underwater; 20% down >40% of a 6-month window), no key rotation (NIP-41 unmerged 3+ years), retention collapse. **SSB**: hash-chained per-author logs give provable retraction ordering and structural spam immunity, but one-key-one-device-forever (second device = permanent identity death), no deletion, and backward-compat paralysis; its own teams wrote the post-mortems and left.
- **ATProto** (42M users): signature-authenticated portable repos work in production; did:plc is a near-complete identity-crux answer (rotation log, plain ECDSA) whose one trust gap — ordering/anti-equivocation — is filled by a central directory, i.e., exactly what a chain provides. Deletion works because repos are current-state; beyond first hop it's policy. Portability is mechanically real and exercised by ~0.2%. Jetstream proved devs strip signatures when verification is expensive.
- **Farcaster**: the closest production precedent to an EFS hybrid — on-chain identity/key registries + off-chain signed messages — and the data half **failed on consensus-on-existence**: CRDT hub sync collapsed (no sync anchor, hours-to-days gossip lag), so they built **Snapchain**, a purpose-built chain (6 validators, 2 companies). Author-from-signature delivered gasless writes in production; storage rent bounded state but not spam (82–91% of paying active accounts spam-labeled; the working filter was a centralized ML classifier).
- **Ceramic** ran EFS's drift-target architecture (chain-free salted IDs, portable signed events, batch Ethereum anchoring as clock) for ~5 years and was deprecated 2025-04: anchors are a **clock, not a consensus** (earliest-anchor ordering unsound under withholding; finality never closes; zero availability); rotatable 3ID produced retroactively-invalid signatures and was replaced by non-rotatable did:pkh.
- **CT/Trillian/KERI/Datomic**: logs are designed to die — data outlives logs via embedded proofs and monitors; witness cosigning (C2SP) replaced never-deployed gossip; verifiable maps (global current-ness) were deprecated; KERI is the direct identity answer (self-certifying key-event logs, pre-rotation, first-seen doctrine) with honest maturity ~one production deployment; Datomic proves retraction-as-new-fact given any total order.
- **VC/DIDs/Verax/Sign**: EAS offchain attestations confirmed non-portable (EIP-712 domain = chainId + per-chain verifyingContract + per-chain version); Verax/Sign counter IDs are maximally anti-LOCKSS; VC revocation (StatusList) hard-fails when the issuer URL dies; the DID ecosystem's correction (did:webvh) independently re-derived KERI; Microsoft abandoned chain-anchored ION.
- **CRDTs/Holochain/Urbit**: lenses + per-author supersession dissolve cross-author merge (BFT-CRDT literature converges on EFS's shape); the one true no-authority concurrency is cross-chain replication — a per-author monotonic counter/prev-hash must be in signed payloads before real data (freeze-adjacent). Holochain: 10 years, key rotation still unstable. Urbit: even sovereignty maximalists anchored identity rotation to Ethereum.
- **On-chain composability**: real but narrow — contracts read point lookups and pre-aggregated facts, never walk trees; 8 of 10 EFS apps need zero contract readers; the two hard categories are same-chain NFT/token metadata bytes and typed-claim gating. Cross-chain proof reads are async, $1–50/query, hard-fork-fragile, vendor-mortal (Axiom dead ~18 months). Replication is EFS's composability mechanism; ENS reversed into cheap L1 for the same reason.
- **Identity crux**: dissolves by splitting *authorization* (live, chain-bound — ERC-1271/4337/7702, the B′ account) from *authorship* (eternal, chain-free — raw-key signatures + key-event log). No scheme makes AA signatures portable. PQ honestly: ECDSA deprecated 2030/disallowed 2035 (NIST); post-CRQC only pre-epoch hash-anchored signatures retain evidentiary value.
- **Spam economics**: gas bundles four functions that unbundle; PoW stamps quantitatively dead (Laurie–Clayton); rent didn't stop spam (Farcaster); what won everywhere = authenticated identity + receiver-side trust scoping — **lenses are the winning mechanism**; the problem reduces to shared-index shape + relay/storage economics.
- **Apps requirements** (10 apps): three write-economics classes (owner-writes / stranger-writes ~$0.001 ceiling / bulk bytes); top requirement = verifiable permanent content identity (7 apps; ~20% of NFT tokenURIs already dead); all revocation needs are CLAIM revocation — no app needs un-signing; portability ranks #6 by count but is the only irretrofittable property and is demanded by the deepest-pocketed personas.
- **Consensus-on-existence**: decomposes into existence/ordering/completeness/equivocation; EFS read semantics need **zero cross-author ordering** (audit-verified) — what EFS actually consumes from a chain is per-author completeness + anti-equivocation. Sharpest hazard: first-attester-wins is anti-monotone under missing data. Three chain-order leaks flagged (LIST caps, SCC tie-break, firstUID).
- **Coupling audit**: post-v2 EAS coupling = 5 mechanisms; waste ≈ 5–10% of write gas; minimal native kernel ≈ 500–900 LoC (less than the EAS it replaces); the deep surface either way is the envelope/replay-domain design; v2 is a one-way hedge cutting substrate-exit cost from "everything" to "claims only."

## Architectures and how they died

- **A — EAS-maximal (chain-carried portable records):** envelope + KEYGRANT log + verified owner-field replication + HEAD/CHECKPOINT cross-chain currency, all in resolver hooks. Red team: single-chain core is genuinely excellent; **cross-chain machinery broken** — HEAD currency circular under withholding (client-managed headSeq + a compromised signer key), earliest-anchor fork choice contradicts the corpus's own Ceramic lesson, full-write replication has no endowment model while cheap CHECKPOINTs are availability-free ("Ceramic's grave"), majority-identity century authenticity degrades to checkpoint-quorum social trust.
- **B — Native kernel:** cleanest engineering; author-from-signature; portable revocation as its raison d'être. Red team: **portable revocation is honest-author-only** (kernel never verifies checkpoint roots; withholding + permissionless replay defeats it); **portable key rotation evaporates under prefix-truncation replay** (a stolen key is an eternal forgery oracle on any chain that never saw the removal); explicit internal contradiction (freshness disclaimed in §8c, sold in §8a).
- **C — Records-first:** only viable century *cost* model; strongest on stranger-writes and hard part (e). Red team: **no distinguished ordering locus** ("chains plural, interchangeable" makes latest-head/key-state/earliest-anchor ill-defined the moment an author touches two chains — fix = mandatory home chain + pre-committed succession, which converges C into D); **completeness keystone availability-naked** (bare state roots = Ceramic's grave; withholding either bricks conforming readers or reopens stale-serving); its headline capability (free tier) is the mission-noncompliant tier.
- **D — Hybrid (records + ingesting kernels):** best-informed synthesis, best doctrines (availability-travels-with-commitment as law; calldata-is-the-artifact; graded currency). Red team: **replay-as-rollback ordering contradiction** (chain-order vs seq-fold — permissionless resubmission of an old record rolls back a slot); **year-100 bundle uncashable on PoS/L2 substrates**; **key windows never close on dead chains + unbounded backdating**; **schedule trips its own kill criterion** (the +2–4-week estimate excluded the KEL registry, anchor accumulator, MST tooling — the exact graveyard: DeepKey 8 years, NIP-41 3 years, PLC needed a Swiss association).

## Judges

- **Archival:** D 6.5 > C 6 > B 4.5 > A 3 (scored direction, post-fix; flips to shipped-A if the window can't absorb any bespoke kernel).
- **Builder:** A 7 > C 5.5 > B 4.5 = D 4.5 ("an archive that exists beats an architecture that never ships").
- **Cypherpunk:** A 6 > B 5.5 > D 5 > C 4 (best floors: consensus-carried availability, self-submission, cold-key EOA year-100 purity; flips to D post-redesign).

## Critic adjudications (carried into [[efs-substrate-decision]])

1. The judges scored different objects; score **what ships in the window** → A-core + mechanical reservations ("v2+ freeze"), D adopted as *direction* (doctrines into Codex prose; machinery only after redesigns + external review).
2. Mission ruling required first: default permanence vs free ephemeral tier — an explicit mission decision, not an architecture side-effect.
3. Earliest-anchor-wins must not appear in frozen semantics (Ceramic lesson adjudicated over the KERI-flavored COPY).
4. Absence/currency claims downgraded system-wide to graded reads (proven-absent vs unknown as normative vocabulary).
5. Record-level seq collisions are never duplicity (device bits mandated); only home-registry equivocation is, and contracts prevent it there.
6. The hedge works only if mechanically reserved (bytes32 identity word, envelope/KEL formats + vectors, reserved schema IDs) — reservation ≠ retrofit.
7. Gas-as-spam-defense struck from A's ledger (phantom weight).
8. Gaps commissioned: Architecture E (witness quorum), bulk-bytes/endowment (Arweave autopsy), illegal-content/liability (zero corpus coverage), privacy/HNDL, substrate mortality + sequencer censorship, Codex governance, one-freeze pledge scope.
9. False-confidence register (single-sourced constants, never-executed procedures, imitation-propagated claims) — kept live in the decision doc §9.
