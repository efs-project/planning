## 0. What this pass was, and the verdict

The dedicated adversarial privacy round [[privacy]] §10 called for: 8 research/design lanes (stealth, ZK, read-path, autopsies, Layer-1 cryptography, metadata adversary, OS private tier, law/positioning), 4 red teams, a binding critic, and a repair round after two agents died mid-flight (the OS-tier lane and the second cryptographer — both replaced; the OS-tier replacement was itself re-attacked, and the replacement cryptographer red team found the round's one freeze defect). **The pass held.** Two round-1 critic rulings were overturned by the repair round (F-2, pulled from the ceremony; the arm-(B) deterministic-FEK sketch, withdrawn — kill #21), one new FATAL was found and repaired by gating (the shred-ring concurrency hole), and the rest is tightening plus honest de-claiming.

**The headline, and it is the best possible answer to "don't paint ourselves into a corner": privacy demands almost no frozen surface.** No new record kind, no new kernel state, no envelope change, no new reserved row. The entire ceremony delta is one pinned derivation function (F-1/A-1: blinded-name formula + domain constant + golden vectors) plus four row-text amendments (F-3, F-4, F-5, R5-zk), riding batches already being cut, plus two optional James calls (the stealth genesis announce line, JD-8; the F-2 subtree-unlock option, JD-36). Both of [[privacy]] §9's "reserve now or never" hedges — a stealth derivation domain and a ZK commitment/nullifier row — were **killed as freeze items**: the kernel never recomputes stealth or ZK math, so both are client-side and Durable. The linchpin that makes deferral safe everywhere else is **F-5**: the `encryptionKey` row's blob is frozen as an *open-ended typed-multi-key* structure, so scan keys, stealth meta-addresses, and post-quantum KEMs are all post-freeze-addable blob entries.

## 1. The privacy canon (PC-1 … PC-14)

**PC-1 — The two-layer frame stands, sharpened.** Layer 1 (confidentiality) is real and now cryptographer-reviewed twice; Layer 2 (metadata/graph) is bounded by *choice* — the price of verify-don't-trust + hyperlinkable + composable. The canonical residual sentence (metadata-adversary §6, promoted to primary positioning text): *"who you write with, when, how much, and how your circle changes over time — is visible by construction, because it is the same structure that lets anyone verify the archive without trusting an indexer."* EFS is **confidential and honestly-bounded, never anonymous**.

**PC-2 — The launch tiering (what ships when).** At launch: **private files** (born-encrypted DATA + X-Wing wraps + committing AEAD), **wrapped sharing**, **encrypted dirnodes as private folders**, config/lens-state roaming (P9), crypto-shred (single-device interim, see PC-4). Post-freeze activation tier: the **salted-TAGDEF family** (addressable/disclosable private anchors — its resolver is reserved, not live). Roadmap: stealth fleets (self-derived ships SDK-side whenever; announced stealth needs JD-8), ZK conventions, PIR. **Marketing must never promise day-one salted private folders, unlinkable pen-names, or anonymous proofs** (positioning markup #1).

**PC-3 — Private folders = encrypted dirnodes (default), salted trees = the reserved tier.** A private folder is ONE encrypted DATA whose padded content is the child table: zero new frozen surface, ships at launch, leaks less than a salted tree (one object's existence + edit cadence + padded size vs full topology + per-node timing), costs O(1) records per folder, and subtree sharing = handing out child caps (no rename-re-keying). Salted TAGDEFs remain reserved for the post-freeze *addressable/disclosable* tier (contract-readable private anchors, reveal-later embargoes) — with F-1 completing their derivation math and F-2 explicitly NOT promising subtree bulk-unlock (JD-36).

**PC-4 — The private tier splits: `private-recoverable` vs `private-shreddable`.** "Recover everything after total device loss" and "crypto-shred means truly gone" are mutually exclusive for one key hierarchy. Recoverable = phrase/escrow-backed, never shreddable; shreddable = enclave-bound high-entropy keys, never escrowed, total loss = permanent loss. User picks per file/subtree; default recoverable. Three structural corollaries: passphrase-derived shred-roots are disqualified; **shared/team content is recoverable-only, forever** (curator-root-derived epochs are always re-derivable); the shreddable tier is **gated behind JD-31** (the shred-ring needs a single-writer discipline — deterministic convergence is structurally impossible for a key that must be destroyable; interim: shreddable ships single-device-only). Launch is not blocked.

**PC-5 — The crypto substrate (blessed, twice-reviewed).** Committing AEAD is a MUST for the private tier — encrypt-then-HMAC-SHA-256 STREAM with `dekCommit = HKDF-SHA-256(DEK, ∅, "efs/v2/dek-commit"‖fileId)` in the prologue AD and the wrap's HPKE aad; it survived all three transplant/replay/equivocation games and is the strongest-specified part of Layer 1 (two mechanisms, documented as two: dekCommit = wrap↔content binding; EtM-HMAC = content-equivocation prevention). Wraps: **X-Wing (X25519+ML-KEM-768), HPKE base mode, single-shot Seal**, `info = "efs/v2/keywrap"‖granter‖fileId`, launch KEM registry `0x01 = xwing-v1`, no classical-only tag ever registered (governance + a conforming-client refuse rule — NOT a frozen invariant). Padding: Padmé + 4 KiB floor + fixed VAL buckets (8 KiB bucket sized under `MAX_VALUE_BYTES` incl. AEAD overhead; sensitive dirnodes may pad to a fixed size because bucket-crossing broadcasts folder growth). Groups: pairwise wraps + team-key indirection to ~50 members; **no MLS/TreeKEM state on-chain, ever** (needs a total order EFS lacks; permanent public commits burn the property that is its point).

**PC-6 — The quantum-expiry honesty line (MUST, positioning + row docs).** X-Wing's confidentiality is a robust combiner (holds if EITHER half holds — post-quantum content secrecy); its anonymity is a weak combiner (needs BOTH — recipient unlinkability is **classical-only** and retroactively falls at CRQC, and scan keys are deliberately classical). Verbatim: *"EFS wraps give post-quantum content secrecy and classical-only recipient unlinkability. Who-shared-with-whom is protected against today's adversaries and is retroactively exposed to a future quantum adversary. Anonymity was never promised; even confidential-tier recipient privacy has a quantum expiry."* This also broadens JD-9's pricing: the ordinary keyWrap recipient graph — not just stealth view keys — is retro-linkable at CRQC.

**PC-7 — Scan/discovery is ONE problem with one convention.** keyWrap recipient discovery and stealth announcement scanning unify: incremental cursor (always) + PRF-derived pairwise mailboxes for known counterparties (zero-leak; counter roams in P9 state or re-derives by self-scan) + view-tag scan for stranger shares (H1, k=16; no birthday/intersection leak — fresh per-wrap ephemerals; false positives cost compute, leak nothing) + owner self-escrow index (F-4) so recovery never replays history. Occurrence keys are frozen as **"opaque"** (computationally unlinkable to recipient identity by non-holders), with random as the degenerate case — the one word (F-3) that keeps all of this conforming. `scanRoot` is a long-lived, not-freely-rotatable secret (custody note + generation list ride JD-11's recovery docs).

**PC-8 — Stealth: RESERVE-shaped, mostly Durable, with the lens tension solved.** A stealth address is an ordinary secp256k1 EOA; recovered-signer authorship means **zero kernel change**, and EFS's signer≠submitter relay model closes the gas-funding linkage that deanonymized ~48% of Umbra mainnet payments (residual: the submitter/relayer learns IP/timing; MUST: relayers never batch one user's fleet in one tx). **Self-derived fleets** are the blessed default — no announcements, no scanning, seed-recoverable, and NOT quantum-retro-linkable (no DH on chain); **announced stealth** is a rare-invite primitive (announced linkage IS retro-linkable at CRQC — "time-locked pseudonymity"). The lens tension resolves as: fleet trust lives in per-viewer **client-config lenses** (explicit carve-out from read-lens LC2's publish rule — JD-25); shared workspaces publicly name only the container author; fleet maps are encrypted CONTENT, never claims (a private persona-link ROW is rejected — a slot keyed on the primary leaks persona existence/count, the A1 oracle applied to ourselves). Meta-addresses ride F-5's blob (no dedicated row needed); the ONLY now-or-never piece is the optional genesis `/.well-known/stealth/announce` line WITH an epoch/time-bucket field (scan-DoS repair) — JD-8.

**PC-9 — ZK: narrow-yes as conventions, zero rows, and a corrected premise.** The narrow-yes list (all Durable): group-publisher pattern at year-0, sibling-contract verifiers for gated actions (never in the kernel; a broken circuit must be replaceable — with the honest note that redeploy protects the archive, not a consuming contract's prior state), proof-in-record-body verified client-side (portable — the circuit is the standard, not the contract), single-user blind-index, nullifier-as-mirror semantics for monotone advisories. ZK state never gates admission (commentary on the master confluence invariant, not new surface). The broad-no: shielded-pool-for-files forfeits the legible, hyperlinkable, composable archive that is the product. **Corrected premise: Fileverse's `zk-granular-permissions` contains no ZK** — it is vOPRF + Merkle-as-key-derivation + AES (verified from source); the production ZK-membership reference is Semaphore, and EFS's opaque-occurrence-key + high-entropy-`encryptionKey` design already dominates the OPRF variant on EFS's threat model.

**PC-10 — Read path: the ladder, and no freeze surface.** The dominant real-world leak is the default RPC provider (sees every query + IP). The ladder: full local replica ("cypherpunk mode," the endgame — size math says feasible for years at plausible adoption) > scoped snapshots (P8's floor) > OHTTP-fronted RPC (client half shippable; **no operational relay/gateway pair exists or is funded as of 2026-07** — dark until sustained) > raw RPC with a no-silent-default picker + embedded light client (integrity, not privacy). Cryptographic PIR: real (Apple-class deployments), fits point-reads, needs zero frozen surface, **shelved with a named trigger** (slot DB > ~5–10 GB or a phone-first client without local snapshots; ~5–7 q/s per 16-core box at 30 GB is the honest number). Adversarially confirmed: no discovery-index or postings-layout choice forecloses PIR or snapshots — read-path freeze delta is NONE.

**PC-11 — What Layer 2 still leaks (the honest ledger).** Even with everything above: wrap fan-out degrees and membership-change deltas (re-key storms broadcast "someone was removed" + the new count), envelope co-batching (MUST: separate envelopes per privacy tier), TID microsecond timestamps (`claimedAt=0` is NOT timing privacy — `order` carries the clock; the real lever is per-tier TID coarsening at a supersession cost, JD-22; `admittedAt` is an unfuzzable coarse floor), device-bit fingerprints, dirnode edit cadence + padded-size transitions, funding provenance (personas need sponsored/relayed flush — a private link alone collapses, JD-20), and the relay's network-layer view of a collaborating clique (off-chain twin of the on-chain leak — stated as loudly). Strongest under-sold win: **reads leave no on-chain trace** (atime is gone) — with the honest caveat that RPC reads leak off-chain instead.

**PC-12 — Collaboration: async-first, spec'd, with serialization law.** The collab-transport spec (os-private-tier §5.6): client-encrypted CRDT deltas over a stateless ciphertext-only relay, session key wrapped to the folder KEK, checkpoints land as normal private DATA under per-file DEKs; async collab ships at launch, live relay is a resourcing decision (JD-30). The correctness law the red teams forced: **all epoch rotations (team keys AND the shred ring) are read-your-latest-epoch serialized** — a rotation prepared from a stale epoch is refused and rebased (JD-33); rosters are LWW-by-rotation-order with monotone removes, **never OR-set union** (union re-admits concurrently-removed members; add-wins stays correct for dirnode child entries only); live sessions re-announce the session key under the new epoch on rotation, with the best-effort residual stated (a removed member retains live-session access until the session ends — JD-34); team-folder reads are causally complete (per-member pointer slots + causally-concurrent superseded versions, causal-frontier bound — C-M) or the invisibility window is documented as an accepted limit.

**PC-13 — PSI (ruled, closing the last [[privacy]] §6 line).** *"PSI adds nothing over the existing recipient-privacy design: random/opaque occurrence keys plus published high-entropy `encryptionKey` blobs leave no low-entropy identifier for an intersection protocol to operate on — the oracle PSI would guard against is already closed by construction (A1/C6/F-3). The OPRF-hardened variant earns one narrow home: the OS-level 'share to an email address/human name' flow, where a low-entropy identifier genuinely exists (frontier-zk §3(a)). No reservation, no convention beyond that flow."*

**PC-14 — Positioning: blessed with four markups.** The law lane's README paragraph, one-liners (*"Confidential when you choose it. Public by default. Anonymous never."* / *"EFS can keep your bytes secret; it cannot keep your existence secret. Design as if the graph shape is visible — because it is."*), FAQ answers, and banned-words list (never "anonymous," "untraceable," "GDPR-compliant," "censorship-proof," "your data is safe," "forward secrecy," and never implying a hardware wallet protects the private tier) ship as official words with: (1) the launch-availability caveat (PC-2), (2) tier honesty (shred claims attach to `private-shreddable` only), (3) the PC-1 residual sentence as the primary privacy-page paragraph, (4) the PC-6 quantum-expiry line. Two structural disclosures join it: the **hardware-wallet gap** (the coupling rule forces the encryption root into software; a HW wallet secures authorship, NOT confidentiality — unfixable, must be said; JD-38) and the GDPR posture (off-chain + commitment is *the* pattern for others' personal data; crypto-shred described as the strongest-available erasure-equivalent, contested as legal erasure — JD-17).
