## The one-paragraph result

**No new record kind, no new kernel state, no new reserved row, no envelope change.** The entire privacy ceremony delta is **one pinned derivation function (A-1: formula + fixed-width domain constant + golden vectors — ROW-class derivation math) plus four row-text amendments (A-3/A-4/A-5/A-6)**, all riding batches already being cut (the D3/D4 salted family, the C3 `encryptionKey` row, the E5 keyWrap row), plus two optional James items. Every ceremony text below is **RED-TEAMED** (attack-layer1, repair round) — none freezes on a single reviewer. The two hedges [[privacy]] §9 flagged as now-or-never (stealth derivation domain; ZK commitment/nullifier row) are killed as freeze items: the derivation domain is client-side math the kernel never recomputes (Durable); the commitment/nullifier row is rejected outright (ordinary kinds + read-side/sibling verification cover it). *Label note: the A-1…A-6 labels below are this sheet's normalization of the pass's F-1…F-5 + R5-zk (mapping stated per item); they are distinct from [[fs-pass-freeze-reservations]]'s own §A item numbers — reconcile at the merge.*

## A. The ceremony batch (adopt — exact frozen text)

**A-1 (was F-1) — blinded-name function, completing D3/D4.** Pin `blindedName = keccak256(DOMAIN_NAME_BLIND_V1 ‖ salt32 ‖ utf8(nfc(name)))` + the constant + golden vectors, in the D3/D4 batch, with four pins:
1. the name bytes are the **UTF-8 encoding of the NFC form**, stated;
2. `name` MUST validate under the **E4 path-segment reject-set + `MAX_NAME_BYTES = 255` *before* blinding** (the salted-family NFC-validation variant is E4's full set; boundary cases in the vectors) — a salted anchor may never commit to a name illegal as a plaintext path (else disclosure/promotion cannot round-trip);
3. `DOMAIN_NAME_BLIND_V1` is **fixed-width**, and the golden-vector suite asserts **pairwise distinctness across all keccak domain constants** (`DOMAIN_CLAIM_V1`, `DOMAIN_LIST_V1`, `DOMAIN_ANCHOR_SALTED`, the `efs.id.tagdef.v1` family, the propertyId domain);
4. salted tagIds inherit **ID-SHAPE-1** (digest-shaped, never address-shaped), stated in the family text.

`salt32` is an author-chosen 32-byte **free input** (random, or deterministically derived per D3's HKDF-salts permission); salt provenance is never verified on-chain. *(Why it must freeze: without the pinned function, disclosures are unverifiable and every client forks the private-path math — D3/D4 as reserved are insufficient.)*

**A-2 (was F-2) — PULLED from the ceremony; the frozen family text carries the negative sentence.** The frozen D3/D4 family text MUST state: *"Per-segment and subtree key derivation is capability-holder convention (Durable). The family does not define — and holders MUST NOT assume — that possession of a node's key or salt yields any descendant's or sibling's key or salt; cross-client partial-disclosure interop is guaranteed only for single-node disclosures (D4 + F-1)."* *(The addendum's exact text; F-1 = A-1 in this sheet.)* Rationale: the previously drafted `K_child = HKDF(K_node, …)` pin does not compose with A-1 (a `K_node` holder has no path to `salt32_child`; the "reveal K_node ⇒ unlock descendants" property is false as specified). Subtree bulk-unlock remains **post-freeze-addable for new trees** via the optional convention **F-2b**: `salt32_child = HKDF-SHA-256(K_node, "efs/v2/salt/anchor" ‖ utf8(nfc(name_child)))[0:32]` (per-tree opt-in; rename re-anchors the subtree; unlocks names-you-know, not blind enumeration). → **JD-36**: James may instead pin F-2+F-2b together at the ceremony (Option A); recommendation is Option B (pull). **Never freeze F-2 alone.** ⚠ F-2b's post-freeze-addability rests on A-1's salt staying a free input and D3's deterministic-salt permission staying intact — if the ceremony narrows either, JD-36 becomes now-or-never.

**A-3 (was F-3) — E5 occurrence-key wording: "opaque," not "random."** Frozen E5 text: private-tier occurrence keys MUST be **opaque — computationally unlinkable to recipient identity by non-holders** (uniformly random is the degenerate case). *(The one word that keeps the scan lanes, owner recovery, and future FMD upgrades conforming; still closes the A1 recipient-oracle, which "random" was shorthand for.)*

**A-4 (was F-4) — E5 self-escrow occurrence key: freeze the PROPERTY only.** Frozen E5 text: *"The reserved self-occurrence-key is opaque, owner-derived from the owner's encryption/scan key branch (never from the identity signing key or its signatures), deterministically re-derivable after total device loss, and never a public constant. The concrete derivation is SDK convention (Durable), version-gated."* The concrete construction (`occ_self = HMAC-SHA-256(scanRoot, "efs/v2/self-escrow" ‖ fileId)`) moves to the conventions registry — self-escrow has no cross-author interop, so the formula must not Etch. *(A public constant would be a permanent O(1) "this file has owner escrow" oracle. Verified: self-escrow wraps are structurally indistinguishable from recipient wraps; occurrence keys are slot coordinates, not wrap targets, so the coupling rule holds.)*

**A-5 (was F-5) — C3 `encryptionKey` blob: freeze the open grammar. THE LINCHPIN.** Frozen C3 text: *"The VAL blob is a typed multi-key structure — entries {algoTag, role, keyBytes, …} with roles ⊇ {kem, scan}; the entry grammar and the KEM algoTag registry are open-ended (Durable, post-freeze-extensible)."* Everything else about the registry is convention (§C below). *(This is what makes stealth meta-addresses, scan keys, recovery-KEM entries, and post-quantum KEMs post-freeze-addable — the reason no dedicated stealth row needs to freeze; the ZK commitment/nullifier row and the stealth derivation domain are killed on independent grounds (kill-list #2/#3). If the frozen text said only "encryption key(s)," every later addition would be a squat on a frozen row's stated meaning.)*

**A-6 (was R5-zk) — KEM-registry key-privacy sentence, with the classical qualifier.** Rides the C3/E5 batch: *"Registry KEM/AEAD suites for private-tier wraps MUST be key-private (anonymity-preserving; no key-id hints in wrap blobs). Classical key-privacy is the launch floor; post-quantum-anonymous suites are a Durable registry upgrade — no launch-registry suite provides post-quantum recipient unlinkability."* *(Necessary: a non-key-private suite rebuilds the recipient oracle one layer down. The qualifier prevents over-reading: X-Wing anonymity needs both halves and falls at CRQC.)*

## B. Optional ceremony items (James decisions)

**B-1 (JD-8) — the stealth genesis announce line.** One `efs.well-known` TAGDEF (`/.well-known/stealth/announce`); announcements are ordinary TAGs (definitionId = that tagId, VAL layout, opaque occurrence keys MUST); body encoding Durable+versioned **WITH a reserved epoch/time-bucket field position** (the scanner-DoS repair — incremental scanners process only since-cursor; reserving the feed without the field forfeits the repair). This is the pass's **only remaining now-or-never**: frozen `efs.well-known` membership can't be added later; a single canonical feed IS the anonymity set. Alternative: skip it — self-fleets need no feed; announced stealth then uses a reshardable post-freeze convention feed (non-canonical discovery forever). Recommend: **reserve WITH the epoch field IF canonical announced stealth is wanted as a product capability, else skip** — the critic explicitly declined an unconditional recommendation and warned against letting the cheap/now-or-never framing drive this call. A dedicated `stealthMeta` row is **DEFER, not reject**: not needed (rides A-5's blob) and not now-or-never as a capability, but the ROW itself is mintable only at the ceremony — James may still mint it as a clean Schelling point (red-team-verified sufficient); recommendation: don't, use the blob (critic C-C). Do NOT mint a stealth schemeTag registry row (Durable) or a derivation-domain constant (Durable).

**B-2 (JD-36) — F-2 Option A.** Only if James wants ceremony-pinned subtree bulk-unlock: freeze F-2+F-2b **together** with vectors (accepting rename-re-anchors-the-subtree). Recommend against (see A-2).
